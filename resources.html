MLA Formatting Guide

MLA (Modern Language Association) is used in English, literature, and humanities courses. Here's everything you need to format your paper and cite your sources correctly.

01

Paper Formatting Basics

How your MLA paper should look before you start writing

Font
Times New Roman, 12pt (or any readable serif)
Margins
1 inch on all sides
Spacing
Double-spaced throughout — including Works Cited
Header
Your last name + page number, top right (e.g., Smith 3)
Heading
Your name · Instructor · Course · Date (top left, 4 lines)
Title
Centered, regular font — no bold, no underline, no larger size
Indentation
First line of each paragraph indented 0.5 inches (one tab)
Works Cited
Starts on a new page; hanging indent (0.5") for each entry
02

In-Text Citations

How to cite sources within your paper

Basic citation (author + page)
According to Smith, writing is a process (42).
Use when you name the author in your sentence. The page number goes in parentheses.
No author named in sentence
Writing is described as a process of discovery (Smith 42).
When you don't name the author, include their last name in the parenthetical.
Block quote (quotes over 4 lines)
Indent the entire quote 0.5 inches. Do not use quotation marks. The period goes before the citation. (Smith 56)
Block quotes are indented. The parenthetical citation comes after the final period.
No page number (web source)
Research confirms this pattern (Johnson).
If no page number exists, use just the author's last name.
03

Works Cited Entries

How to format your sources list at the end of your paper

📚 Book
Last, First. Title of Book. Publisher, Year.
Morrison, Toni. Beloved. Knopf, 1987.
📰 Journal Article
Last, First. "Article Title." Journal Name, vol. #, no. #, Year, pp. ##–##.
Davis, Angela. "Prison Reform." Social Justice Quarterly, vol. 12, no. 3, 2019, pp. 45–62.
🌐 Website
Last, First. "Page Title." Website Name, Date Published, URL.
Jordan, June. "Poem for South African Women." Poetry Foundation, 12 Mar. 2021, www.poetryfoundation.org/poems/example.
📺 Video / YouTube
Last, First (or Channel Name). "Video Title." YouTube, Date, URL.
TED. "The Danger of a Single Story." YouTube, 7 Oct. 2009, www.youtube.com/watch?v=D9Ihs241zeg.
📖 Chapter in Edited Book
Last, First. "Chapter Title." Book Title, edited by First Last, Publisher, Year, pp. ##–##.
Baldwin, James. "Notes of a Native Son." The Price of the Ticket, edited by Maya Angelou, St. Martin's Press, 1985, pp. 127–145.
💡 Hanging Indent Tip: In your Works Cited, the first line of each entry is flush left. All following lines are indented 0.5 inches. In Word: highlight entries → Paragraph → Indentation → Special → Hanging.

Grammar & Mechanics

Common Grammar Mistakes

These are the errors tutors see most often. Learn to spot and fix them, and your writing will immediately become clearer.

Error

Comma Splice

❌ I went to the library, I studied for three hours.

✅ I went to the library, and I studied for three hours.

Two independent clauses can't be joined with just a comma. Use a conjunction, semicolon, or period.

Error

Run-On Sentence

❌ The assignment was hard I asked for help it was useful.

✅ The assignment was hard. I asked for help, and it was useful.

Break up long run-ons into separate sentences or properly connected clauses.

Error

Sentence Fragment

❌ Because she studied every night.

✅ Because she studied every night, she passed the exam.

A fragment is missing a subject, verb, or complete thought. Every sentence must stand alone.

Watch Out

Vague Pronoun Reference

❌ The teacher told the student that she needed to revise.

✅ The teacher told Maria that Maria needed to revise her essay.

Make sure every pronoun clearly refers to one specific noun.

Watch Out

Subject-Verb Agreement

❌ The group of students are working hard.

✅ The group of students is working hard.

The verb agrees with the subject, not the nearest noun. "Group" is singular.

Tip

Apostrophe Use

❌ The student's essay's were graded.

✅ The students' essays were graded.

Apostrophes show possession (student's paper) or contractions (it's = it is). Never for plural nouns.

Essay Writing

Essay Structure & Strategy

A well-structured essay is easier to write and easier to read. Here are the building blocks every essay needs.

1

Introduction

Start with a hook (a question, surprising fact, or bold statement). Build context. End with a clear thesis statement.

⚡ Your intro should be a funnel — broad at the top, narrowing to your specific argument.
↓
2

Body Paragraphs (TEEL)

Topic sentence → Evidence → Explanation → Link back to thesis. One main idea per paragraph.

⚡ If your paragraph can support two different topic sentences, split it into two paragraphs.
↓
3

Conclusion

Restate your thesis in fresh words. Summarize your main points. End with a "so what?" — why does this argument matter?

⚡ Never introduce new evidence in your conclusion. Synthesize what you've already proven.

Writing a Strong Thesis Statement

Most Important Skill

Your thesis is the spine of your entire essay. A weak thesis leads to a weak essay. A strong thesis is arguable, specific, and provable.

❌ Too Weak / Obvious

"Social media has both positive and negative effects on students."

Why it fails: It's not arguable. No one would disagree.

✅ Strong Thesis

"Excessive social media use undermines students' academic performance by fragmenting attention and replacing deep reading with passive scrolling."

Why it works: It takes a specific, arguable position with clear reasoning.

💡 Tips from Our Writing Consultants

  • Always read your paper aloud before submitting — your ear catches errors your eyes miss.
  • Your thesis should answer the question: "So what?" If it doesn't, revise until it does.
  • Every body paragraph should directly support your thesis. If it doesn't, cut or rewrite it.
  • Visit the Writing Center early — don't wait until the night before a paper is due.
  • Revision is not proofreading. Real revision means rethinking your argument, not just fixing typos.
  • When stuck, try "free writing" — write nonstop for 10 minutes without editing. It breaks writer's block.
